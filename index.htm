Company Formation in Dubai

One of the biggest cities in the Middle East is Dubai, which provides a wide range of business opportunities for the establishment of various types of firms by international investors. To ensure that trade and business in the region continue to grow, this city has increased its opportunities. In addition, Dubai has diversified its economy so that less than 1% of its total GDP comes from oil output. The speed of Dubai's diversification and long-term economic growth has quickened. Business-friendly government policies and programmes, tax structures, and infrastructure, to name a few, are the key drivers of the expansion. It is clear from the discussion above that Dubai is a great site to start business in Dubai, and that business owners, especially international investors, can benefit much from forming a firm here.

Process

Choosing business structure

An LLC, branch office, joint stock company, free zone company, or trade representative office are among the legal forms that may be used. The number of shareholders, the commercial activities pursued, and the company's capital requirements for company formation in Dubai all influence the choice of an appropriate business structure.

Reserving name for the business

This is the name under which the business will operate. The applicant should check with the relevant Free Zone Authority or the Department of Economic Affairs of the UAE to see if the chosen name is available. The applicant must confirm that the name they have selected is available and has not previously been registered by another business.

Obtaining the necessary business licenses

Obtaining the necessary business licenses, registrations, and pre-approvals from the relevant authorities. In order to receive pre-approvals, business registration, and the proper business license from the relevant authorities, enterprises must apply with the appropriate paperwork.

Deciding location of the company:

The decision related to the office's location can be a game-changer for starting business in Dubai. The office space selection depends on the kind of business activity chosen, the number of employees hired and the budget allocated for the same which can be either buy or lease the land.

Dubai business license:

The dubai business license is the legal document that grants its owners the right to engage in the designated commercial activity. Commercial, industrial, and professional licenses are some of the several licensing categories.

Opening a bank account

After obtaining the necessary registrations and approvals from the relevant authorities, the business is required to operate a corporate bank account in its name for the purpose of receiving and transferring funds to advance its business affairs.

Eligiblilty

Limited Liability Company (LLC)

In this type of business structure, the shareholders' liability to the company is restricted to the amount of their share capital contributions. The applicant needs sponsorship from a UAE national or a fully owned UAE company to act as a national agent or sponsor in order to establish an LLC in Dubai. There is no minimum capital requirement, however it should be at least AED 100,000 and have one management or director. For company formation in Dubai, the business is required to have a legal reserve equal to 10% of the LLC's net profits.

Joint stock company (JSC)

A partner in a joint stock company(JSC) in Dubai is solely responsible for the amount of capital they contributed to the business's share capital. In Dubai, there are two different kinds of JSC: private joint stock and public joint stock company. A minimum of 2-15 directors who are chosen for a three-year term are required for a JSC firm setup in Dubai. Atleast 10 founders and a minimum share capital of AED 10,000,000 are required for a public joint stock company, while atleast 3 founders and share capital of AED 2,000,000 are required for a private joint stock company.

Companies in the Free Zone

Unlike mainland Dubai, the Free Zone has its own set of rules on ownership, ownership rights, taxation, and other matters. Free Zone Company and Free Zone Establishment are the two types of businesses that can be founded there. A Free Zone LLC may have up to five shareholders. A Free Zone Establishment may have up to 1 shareholder with limited liability.

Branch office with a dual license

Branch office with a dual license that is an outpost of a free zone business on the mainland. These offices possess a second license in addition to the free zone license, which lowers the expense and time required to setup company in the UAE. It is ineligible to provide labor cards since it cannot be registered with the UAE Ministry of Labor.
